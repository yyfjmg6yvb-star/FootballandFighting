Bare Knuckle Fighting Championship (BKFC) is the world's leading bare knuckle fighting promotion, founded in 2018 by David Feldman. It's exactly what it sounds like — fighters compete without boxing gloves, using only hand wraps on the wrists (not the knuckles).

The Rules

Fights take place in a circular pit called "The Squared Circle" — actually a small circle 22 feet across, keeping fighters close. No grappling, no clinching, no takedowns. Pure stand-up striking only. Five rounds of two minutes each. No hitting behind the ear or back of the head.

Why it's growing fast

BKFC has grown from a niche novelty into a legitimate sport with a proper broadcast deal and household names. It attracted fighters from boxing (Paulie Malignaggi, Artem Lobov), MMA (Paige VanZant, Joe Riggs), and WWE. The action is intense — cuts are common and the pace is relentless because there's nowhere to hide.

UK following

BKFC has built a strong following in the UK through BT Sport coverage. Several British fighters have competed under the BKFC banner and the promotion has held events in the UK.
